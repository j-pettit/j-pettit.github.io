The Past.

If I ever find the need to write an autobiography, I will detail my early life meticulously. For now, I'll skip some of the more mundane or irrelevant details and jump forward to my teenage years. In 2011 I entered grade 9, and after a four year whirlwind I graduated.

During these years, I discovered many of the interests and goals I still hold today. I dipped my fingers into the pies of many interests, becoming a member of the improvisation club, the trivia team, the student council, and several concert and jazz bands.

By grade 12, the student council had chosen me to be the council's Leadership Head. By that time, I was also the improvisation team's Head and had joined the environmental club and aboriginal student's club, among others.

Unfortunately, all of these groups made the decision of choosing a post-secondary path very difficult. How could I choose just one thing to be, out of a world of possibilities and experiences to be had?

I eventually decided to apply for the University of Waterloo's Mechatronics Engineering program, out of my love for science and technology, but also to give myself a well-rounded and broad educational experience. I was accepted to the program, and In september of 2015 made the move to Waterloo.

The Present.

Right now I'm in my second year of University, or more specifically my 2A term if you're familiar how the uWaterloo system works. I've been taking some amazing courses taught by great professors and TA's, and I'm looking forward to starting my second co-op term in January.

I recently finished my first co-op term at Innovasium Digital, learning the tricks of the web app development trade. This website is my first personal web project, but my time at Innovasium had me working on several applications in use by CIBC, Brookfield, and Upper Canada Forest Products.

Outside of my web development, I've been tinkering with an Arduino microcontroller thermostat project for my apartment. I recently finished creating a Nintendo 64 emulator with a Raspberry Pi application processor, and had a blast playing it with some friends once it was completed.

This term I've rejoined the Waterloo Satellite team and the Waterloo Kendo Club, and I'm looking forward to an exciting semester!
